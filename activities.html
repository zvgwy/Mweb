Activity 2

Silent Reflection

Conversion of Pamplona

I entered college with a plan. Finish this 4-year course, graduate by June 2025, get a job, and live life. There is no problem with dreaming big because that’s how we go on with life, achieving our desires. However, there are times when life will humble you. It was December 2022 when I had a major setback in college which stopped me in my tracks, I failed a major course. My life was in a stall. How do I tell my parents? What about my plan? What now? These were the questions that were going through my mind that time.

Over the months, I had a hard time accepting the fact that I am now an irregular student. I tend to get shy getting along with my new classmates (who are not my batchmates). However, even though I am an irregular student, I don’t feel left out. That was when I realized that maybe God is telling me something. God reminded me that control is an illusion and growth often comes through surrender. This experience taught me to stay grounded and to have humility to accept that not everything goes in my way. And through my inner freedom, I must learn how to pivot things.

Life does not end with a single F grade. Don’t let anyone or anything stop you from dreaming big no matter how turbulent life may be. Always remember, every delay is a redirection.

Activity 3

Silent Reflection

Conversion of Manresa

As an irregular student, I often see the milestones of my batchmates who are now a Registered Chemical Engineer. I am now just a month away from finishing this college degree, and I am supposed to be optimistic and grateful for where I am at the moment. However, I couldn’t help but pause and ask myself, “I’m 24 now and still in college. I am supposed to be earning money at this stage in life. What am I doing?” These are the internal noises that distract me from hearing God’s gentle whisper. This uncertainty that I feel shatters my vision in life. It hides the fact that everything is under control and is progressing.

It is a good thing that through this reflection, I was able to be self-aware of the noises that surround me. It served as God’s sign for me to pause and learn that not everything will go as planned. Being in this situation, I am invited to discern because a single hurdle doesn’t define the rest of your life. I am the navigator of my own life, with God as my compass that lights the path towards success.

Activity 4

Silent Reflection

Conversion of River Cardoner

I have been a student leader since I was a freshman in college, particularly in organizations related to my program. Ever since, I was known for being that one chemical engineering student who excels in this aspect, leadership. A moment that lives in my mind rent free is when I became the resource speaker for the FreChEs Kickoff, an orientation event for 1st year chemical engineering students. When they introduced me prior to my talk, I was greeted with a warm round of applause from the audience when my name was called. Throughout my talk, I felt respected and recognized by everyone which is a full circle moment for me. I realized that I’ve come so far, from being an attendee way back 2021 to becoming the resource speaker this year.

Looking back, I feel proud and grateful for the journey, knowing that my growth in leadership has truly come full circle. It’s a reminder that dedication, perseverance, and passion can turn dreams into meaningful milestones.
