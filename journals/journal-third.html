Third.

This journal reflects on the session last January 30, 2026 entitled Looking Beyond the Self. We were divided into six (6) groups and we are to discuss how DIGNITY is applied or observed in the university environment.

Of all the themes, concepts, and content in the presentation on Looking beyond the Self, which do you think you have learned about the most? What do you think could be the reason it has left a mark in you until now?

Being Intentional with the Ignatian Values

I have learned the most about the 7 values presented during the session. It has left a mark on me until now as I realized how transactional my life in the university is that I forgot the importance of practicing these values as we interact with people. As I look back, I may have been unconsciously practicing some of the values but I realized how important it is to recognize them and be intentional with becoming men and women for others as an atenean.

Can you recall a particular instance, class, activity, or engagement you had that made you learn more about that concept or theme? What kind of transformation did you have or are you having since that moment?

A More Humane Life

We had a group activity where we reflected on how the value of dignity is observed in our daily lives in the university. This activity made me understand the theme, Looking beyond the Self, as an opportunity to reflect on how we are as an AdDU student. Do we practice the Ignatian values instilled in us in our university lives? The gist of our group’s reflection is that we recognized dignity specifically in how we treat others. We respect everyone in the university, the staff, faculty, and students, as they have dignity. We recognized their inherent worth as a person; hence, we give a fair treatment to everyone. This realization helped me treat myself with more compassion, especially during moments of pressure. Moreover, I grew more attentive to the dignity of others, becoming less judgmental. Hence, as I uphold this value when interacting with anyone in the university and with myself, I am living a life that is more humane.

Who are the people in your AdDU journey whom you are most thankful for? As you move forward, how do you plan to deepen this value you have nurtured? How do you think it may help you and the society as well?

Practicing Gratitude Throughout Life

Firstly, I am thankful to my family for their dedication in providing my financial and material needs as I study at the Ateneo de Davao University. Secondly, my classmates and friends for the genuine connections I built with them along the way. Studying BS Chemical Engineering in a competitive university is such an overwhelming journey. With my friends, they never once let me feel that I am alone and they treated me with dignity and respect. I am also thankful to the professors I have encountered in my stay in AdDU as they have helped me improve my skills necessary for me to survive my college program. Lastly, I am thankful for my partner who has been with me through thick and thin. Her encouragement, kind words, and unwavering support made me feel safe and assured that this challenging journey will be worthwhile in the end. As I move forward, I plan to deepen the value of gratitude by recognizing them throughout my life. Maintaining our genuine connection through random check-ins or catch-ups signifies their importance in my life. This value will help me grow as a person who leads with dignity and respect, and in society, could contribute through collaboration and service.
