Fourth.

From navigating online learning during the pandemic to embracing leadership, I could tell how much I grew over the past five (5) years. The journey map that I made allowed me to stay grounded as I revisit my experiences and milestones. I never knew making the journey map (Life in AdDU) would be a wholesome moment for me who is now a senior in college. With that, this journal entry reflects on how AdDU has formed me, where I encountered God along my journey, and how I am being called to continue my life journey moving forward.

How have I realized about my AdDU journey while making my AdDU Journey Map?

The Journey of Growth

As I created my journey map, I realized how much I have grown beyond academics. As a student who started my freshman year online, lost and unsure with the path I took, I did not expect that I would find myself serving the ChE community with pride and passion. However, my journey wasn’t linear as I had my shared rock-bottom moments from failed exams and overwhelming pressure during leadership and academic work. Yet looking back, I was meant to experience those as it contributed to who I am now. Overall, mapping my AdDU journey allowed me to acknowledge my growth over the years and assured myself that I am meant to take this life path.

What does this say about my education or formation in AdDU?

A Holistic Educational Practice

Studying in AdDU clearly tells about their holistic approach in honing their students, an education that goes beyond technical competence. Despite the challenging program I took, AdDU has taught me how to lead while upholding the Ignatian values; most importantly, serving others with commitment. Through organizations like JPIChE and PIChE-JCM, I learned responsibility, teamwork, and trust because leadership is not meant to be carried alone. Through academics, I learned to practice discernment and acknowledging God in all things. Hence, AdDU has become a huge part of shaping me as an individual.

Like St. Ignatius’s experience as a pilgrim, where have I found God in my journey?

Everything Will Make Sense. Even If You Think It Doesn’t

Honestly, I have only started to find God in my journey during my rock-bottom moments. There have been a lot of times where I struggled balancing my academics and leadership responsibilities. I failed some of my exams, I settled for mediocrity in my deliverables in JPIChE. I lost my spark to live my life but through the company of my friends and loved ones, I managed to love life again. They served as God’s instruments to remind me that I am strong enough to face my hurdles in life and that everything will make sense in the end — I just needed to acknowledge God’s presence and trust Him. From then, I am reminded that God was present in my life all along.

Looking back, how am I being called to continue my life journey?

Navigate My Career with Humility

My experiences in AdDU have called me to apply the holistic approach as I build my career. I am called to lead with humility and perform my duties with commitment. I am called to treat any discomfort and unfamiliarity in my work as an opportunity to grow while upholding the Ignatian teachings. As I move forward, I am reminded to find God in all things as St. Ignatius does.
