Sixth.

As I enter the final stretch of my college life, I find myself reflecting on the importance of discernment in my journey. Discernment has been a guiding light for me, helping me navigate through the uncertainties and challenges that come with this phase of life. It has taught me to pause, reflect, and make decisions that align with my values and aspirations. In this journal entry, I want to explore how discernment has been a crucial part of my growth and how it can continue to shape my path as I prepare to become a professional.

How can I grow more in caring for my whole self, so I can also care for others?

Becoming More Human Through Care

As a person who grew up with bombarded demands, I get caught up in responsibilities and expectations that I forget to check in with myself. Over the years, I realized that I’ve been serving others not out of willingness but out of responsibility. This is unhealthy as I see the people as more important than my wellbeing. As the discussion has said, serve where you can serve well. I’m realizing that I can’t give fully to others if I’m already drained. Caring for myself means allowing rest without guilt, setting boundaries, and being honest about what I’m feeling, and there is nothing bad about that.

When I am more patient and understanding with myself, I become more patient and compassionate toward others. I’m able to give more sincerely, not just because I have to, but because I genuinely want to. Maybe caring for my whole self isn’t a selfish act, rather, it is necessary, so I can show up better for others while still taking care of who I am becoming.

To care for both yourself and others is to live with humanity.
